Office Hour Assistant

Introduction

UIUC CS has wide variety of talent ranging from those who know more than all the instructors combined to those who are still learning to use the terminal. And at some point almost all of these students come to office hours to get extra help. Whether it be drawing figures on the whiteboard or stepping through GDB, the labor of a Office Hour Assistant is priceless. In fact, few things are more rewarding than helping a student understand and to help them develop a passion for the subject you teach.

Skills Required

    Patience and Empathy

    A lot of the times students might ask you questions that you feel are "dumb" or "trivial", but the reality is that we were all there at one point and it is important to constantly empathize and think back to when you were a CS 241 Student. Most of the times, you can fill the gap in a student's understanding of the material just by remembering why you struggled with the material and what helped you understand it. However, some students might not get the material even after the third time you explained it to them. This is where patience comes into play, since you probably just have to think of another way of explaining the material to them.

    Friendly Personality and Demeanor

    Students will most likely come to office hours stressed and upset after debugging for several hours and waiting on a long queue. A lot of this tension is easily alleviated if you approach them nicely and they like you. It is important to never appear condescending or annoyed.

    Communication and Teaching Ability

    Teaching well is much different from doing well in a course. It helps to understand the material well and be comfortable with it, but if you can not explain the material, then you are essentially useless in office hours. You also have to be adaptable in the ways you explain things, since the how you understand the problem might not be how your students best understand a problem.

    Debugging

    It should go without saying that debugging is a critical skill to have when running an office hour. Most of the bugs that can be found during office hours can be easily found by using GDB, Valgrind and print statements. Another important part of debugging is the ability to consider edge cases that your students might not have thought about. And most importantly, you should be able to step through your students logic and help narrow the search for the line of code that is causing everything to go wrong downstream.

    Firm Knowledge of the Assignments Given In the Course

    Students will mainly come into office hours needing help interpreting the MP Docs or debugging code. It is most time efficient if you get familiar with the assignments before you walk into office hours. This way you are quickly able to interpret MP documentation for your students. You should also think of common bugs most your students are going to have and which parts of the assignment they are going to struggle the most on.

Expected Time Obligations

    Team Meetings (1 or 2 hrs / week)

    It is important for a staff as large as CS 241 that everyone who hosts office hours to be familiar with current assignments. This is probably be what the bulk of the team meeting will be spent on. You will probably meet with someone on the MP Development team who will go over the specs of the MP. And in this time the team should at least go over the detailed TA-Solution the MP Team will put together. This time is also useful for going over common bugs that you will encounter. Meetings will also be useful, since more experienced course staff members will give you advice throughout the year.

    Office Hours (3 hrs / week)

    It should go without being said that as an office hour assistant you will be hosting office hours. This is the time where you will put yourselves on the chara queue and help students on the queue. The reason we want about 3 hours a week is because you get better at finding an MPs bugs overtime, so it would be kind of a waste if you only did one hour.

    Piazza (1 hr / week)

    It is also important to answer questions on piazza, since some students are too shy to come to office hours. Also, answering one question well on piazza can potentially resolve the problem for the entire class.

Machine Problem Developer

Introduction

Machine Problems are the meat and cheese of any programming course. These assignments are supposed to be rigorous enough so that students can fully apply their knowledge of systems. And interesting enough, so that they have a memorable experience of the course. Writing Machine Problems involves coming up with a meaningful assignments where students can not only apply their knowledge, but also learn. Writing these assignments involves a lot of time drafting on whiteboards and bouncing ideas. As you are writing these assignments you will have to ask yourself "What am I trying to have the students learn?". Students are very busy and you can not give MPs that take 35 hrs and have them spend a bunch of time doing string manipulation (unless that is the intent of the assignment). Finally, with any good MP comes an excellent documentation that is clear, concise, contextual and correct.

Skills Required

    Firm Knoweledge of C

    Unlike lab assisting and office hours, you will be doing a lot of coding for MP development. It is exponentially harder to write and develop an MP than it is to solve an MP. You will not be provided any utility functions, since you are the one providing them. Everything that was out of the scope of the course is now fair game as a MP developer.

    Solid Understanding of System Programming

    As a MP developer you must really understand the thing you write, since writing a bug in your MP is equivalent to writing 200+ bugs that get deployed to students. Any race conditions or memory leaks left in the provided code you write will be deployed to 200+ students.

    Extensive Knowledge of Linux

    As a MP developer, you will probably have to write makefiles for the class. You will need to understand Linux at a level that was never required of you as a student.

    Goal Driven

    You will need to be efficient with your student's time. You need to set clear learning goals for your students. It is very bad to waste your students time with an MP that is not relevant to the material being taught in the course.

Expected Time Obligations

    Team Meetings (2 hrs / week)

    You will need to meet with the rest of the MP development team frequently. This is so that everyone is on the same page on what the MP is supposed to teach its students. This is also an excellent time for the team to separate out the work for a divide and conquer strategy, since you will almost constantly be up againt a deadline. These meetings will also be an excellent time to learn from course staff more experienced than you. The MP Development team will probably also meet up with the Autograding team to agree on the structure of the MP.

    Development and Testing (5 hrs / week)

    You will need to develop meaningful MPs and doing so takes a lot of labor. Even after the team has written documentation and a TA solution, you will need to test the provided code throughly. This is to ensure the MP is at least doable and hopefully catch any potential bugs.

    Addressing Student Concerns (1 hr / week)

    It would be a miracle for an MP to be deployed without a problem, but the chances of this happening is slim to none. The team will have to accommodate for bugs in the provided code and ambiguity in documentation. This is also an important time to take in student feedback so that the team can improve the MP for future semesters.

Autograding

Introduction

There are many assignments in a programming class and CS 241 is no exception. All these assignments need to be graded and as the field of computing grow so does its students. It is no longer feasible to grade all these assignments by hand or have every student present their code to a T.A. This is where autograding comes into play. Autograding is essentially writing scripts that can take a student's code and run a series of testcases to best measure its correctness and performance. At a high level the process of autograding is as follows:
  • Write testcases and scripts
  • Have script grade students acording to timetable
    • Pull code from SVN
    • Run each testcase
    • Gather statistics like points earned, memory usuage and time
    • Tally everything up
  • Commit grades to gradebook

Skills Required

    Firm knoweledge of Python 2.7

    The autograding infrastructure is written in python and developed for version 2.7. You will most likely be implementing testcases for each MP which uses a lot of python syntax. You might also be extended the autograder itself in which case you will be writing classes and modules. We except a decent level of code style and readability.

    Solid Understanding of System Programming

    A lot of what makes autograding for CS 241 a unique challenge is that your students will be programming at the system level. In no other course will you have to write an autograder where students will be forking, writing to files, and making system calls. Autograding is easy at a high level, since it is just scripting, but nontrivial in CS 241.

    Extensive Knowledge of Linux

    You will probably be doing at lot of development in the linux terminal as an autograder. You must be familiar all the linux commands that you will be having your script use. There is also little to no UI for the autograder and everything must be done in the terminal.

    White Hat Hacking

    There will always be students trying to fake results or beat the autograder. When you write your test cases, you should try and make sure that students can not just hard code the right solutions. There are also malicious students that will try and bring down the autograder, because "some men just want to watch the world burn". You will be responsible for sandboxing your testing.

Expected Time Obligations

    Team Meetings (2 hrs / week)

    You will need to meet with the rest of the Autograding team frequently. This is so that everyone is on the same page on how to test students' code for the MP. This is also an excellent time for the team to separate out the work for a divide and conquer strategy, since you will almost constantly be up againt a deadline. These meetings will also be an excellent time to learn from course staff more experienced than you. The Autograding team will probably also meet up with the MP development team to agree on the structure of the MP.

    Development and Testing (4 hrs / week)

    You will need to develop and implement testcases and doing so takes a lot of labor. Even after the team has developed testcases you will need to test the testers code throughly. This is to ensure that you are accurately testing the correctness and performance of student's code.

    Addressing Student Concerns (1 hr / week)

    It would be a miracle for an MP to be graded without a problem, but the chances of this happening is slim to none. The team will have to accommodate for bugs in the testers and also ensure students that all testers are fair. This is also an important time to take in student feedback so that the team can improve the testcases for future semesters.

Development Team

Introduction

CS 241 is probably the most rich and rigorous of all the intro CS courses. There are so many aspects of the course and so many areas where learning can be enhanced. As the field of computing evolves so do we. Education is no longer a static one directional communication between a professor's black board and a students head. It has become a plethora or brilliant young minds that want to learn skills that will help in their pursuit to becoming the strongest version of themselves. With this comes to opportunity of making educational resources available to students. Last semesters we had a couple of projects which included ClassTranscribe and Markdown2PDF. ClassTranscribe took static lecture videos and transcribed them, so that students can easily find the content they desire. Markdown2PDF took the already popular CS241 WikiBook and automated the process of converting it to a PDF, so that students can have a physical copy of the most up to date wikibook. Little things like these make the learning experience at UIUC memorable and unique. If you have a cool idea that you want to implement for CS 241, then this is the role for you!

Skills Required

    Creativity

    A lot of the things that CS 241 could use to better educate its students has not even been thought of yet. This is something that as member of the development you should think about. Some of the most useful tools that will be made are not things that are difficult to implement, but rather just really creative.

    Desire To Take Education To The Next Level

    CS is a constantly evolving field and so should its education of students. You should be never be completely satisfied with the current state of education and should always want more for your students. If you think there is a way to make education 1% better for 1% of students, then you should pursue it!

    Development Skills For Your Medium

    Regardless of your game changing idea, you will need to be able to express and implement it. If you want to make instructional videos, then you will probably want video editing skills. If you want to make an app that lets kids view transcribed lectures, then you should probably know web programming and databases. If you want to convert a wikibook from markdown to pdf, then you should probably know how to script and latex.

Expected Time Obligations

    Undetermined

    Time will vary with your team and its goals. Some teams might be really proactive and might want to develop a stack over flow for UIUC CS courses and might require a lot of effort and time. Other might want to just make the man pages look better.

Honors Section Developer

Introduction

The honors sections for CS 241 is a lot like anything else in 241; fun, challenging and rewarding. This section will be filled with the university's most talented and most enthusiastic. This translates to honor's students being the most knowledge hungry and our pickiest customers. They will except a section that pushes the bounds of a system programming course and you will be responsible for providing exactly that. Unlike the rest of the course, material and content will be geared toward the A and A+ students. You will interact with students that match and may surpass your intelligence and the thought of this should excite you. If you have a deep passion for systems and want to instill that passion in the course's 1%, then this is the position for you.

Skills Required

    Creativity

    The honors section is all about reaching out to the A and A+ students, so it is up to the staff running this section to develop content for said students.

    Confidence

    The honors section will have the course's most demanding students. These students are self motivated, ambitious, diligent, talented (that's how they got there) and expect the same from you. These standard are intimidating and require confident (and competent) people who are willing to meet them.

    Flexible (Jack of All Trades)

    Unlike the regular section, the honor section will be very streamlined on staff resources. This means, you might not always have people on the autograding staff and mp development to help you. So you have to be able to pick up a lot of skills on the spot.

Expected Time Obligations

    Undetermined

    The honors section is still under heavy development and always open to changes and improvements. The time required of you will vary based on the goals of the course. Time will probably be spent on developing and grading assignments.
